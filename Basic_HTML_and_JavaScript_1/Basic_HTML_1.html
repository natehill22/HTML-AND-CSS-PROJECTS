The word "armadillo" comes from the Spanish word armadillo, which means "little armored one". It is a diminutive of armado, meaning "armored," and was given by Spanish explorers to the animals with their protective bony plates.

Armadillos have peg-like cheek teeth that lack enamel and continuously grow throughout their lives. They do not have front teeth (incisors or canines), and their teeth are used for grinding their insect-heavy diet.

Armadillos can run surprisingly fast for their appearance, with some species, like the nine-banded armadillo found in the United States, reaching top speeds of up to 30 miles per hour. This speed is a primary defense mechanism for many armadillo species, rather than relying on their ability to roll into a ball.
