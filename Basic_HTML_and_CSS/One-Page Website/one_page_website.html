Explorations on Cobbler Variants

There are many different types of desserts based around the delicious explosion of flavor when you cook dough with or within fruit.

Many people, pros even, don't understand the differences between them. Here, we will display several of these desserts and what sets them apart.

Variations:

Cobbler

Dollops of biscuit dough added overtop fruit and oven-baked, forming "cobbles." Cobblers are the most recognized dessert in this category.

Crisp

Fruit baked under a crunchy, streusel-like oat topping.

Grunt

Dollops of dumpling dough added overtop fruit and steam-cooked on the stovetop.

Crumble

Oat-free streusel baked over fruit.

Slump

Dollops of soft dumpling dough added overtop fruit and oven-cooked. Has a tendency to "slump" under heat.

Brown Betty

Fruit and buttered dough crumbs repeatedly layered upon each other and baked.

Buckle

Fruit suspended within cake dough (fruit poured over dough) and baked.

Sonker

Syrup-y cooked fruit under dense pancake batter poured overtop and baked. The fruit in sonkers has a lot of moisture, often due to the addition of moonshine.

Pandowdy

Puff pastry, pie, or pastry dough pressed into fruit and baked.
